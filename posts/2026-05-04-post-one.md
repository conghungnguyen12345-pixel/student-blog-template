Title: What is Critique Canvas and the purpose of it
Date: 2026-05-04
Author: Hung Nguyen
Summary: Explaining the brief of the project and determining the main operating requirements for our platform, where artists can give feedback on designs

## Understanding the Brief

The brief of the assignment requires us to develop a platform for the community that showcases a unique feature that convinces users to choose it, rather than Reddit and Instagram. This approach is crucial since our group avoid common social elements and focuses on a specialised and personal direction. After discussion, our team developed Critique Canvas: a platform where artists upload work and receive feedback attached directly to the image.

## The Problem With Existing Platforms

Most artists who look for feedback on online platforms such as Instagram or Reddit will face a common obstacle: comments are not directly connected to the work. A comment such as "the anatomy feels off in the arm area" will be confusing because now the audience will not know which arm or which specific spot of the arm. This results in conflict, especially for the more complicated artworks with multiple areas for feedback.

## Distinguishing Core From Optional Requirements

This issue requires deeper scoping. After analysing what the platform cannot function without and what would improve it, we divided the requirements into two tiers:

**Essential features that the app will fail if lack:**
- Artwork upload with persistent storage
- Click-to-annotate: capturing coordinates on image click and saving them to the database
- Annotation pins rendered on the image at the correct position
- Accessible fallback list of all annotations below the image
- Annotations tied to the logged-in user, so feedback has authorship

**Valuable but not crucial:**
- Category tagging (composition, lighting, anatomy, etc.)
- Filtering annotations by category
- Deleting your own annotations

This divergence is crucial because it helps the scope to be realistic. If our project aims to deliver a wide variety of things, it might affect the quality and the reliability of each feature. 

## A Constraint That Shapes Everything

One technical issue occurred early that affects both the database design and the UI: if we store annotation coordinates as pixel values, pins will appear in the wrong position whenever the image appears at a different scale. A pin saved at pixel 340,220 on a 1200px-wide display will be completely misaligned on a 600px-wide screen.

The solution is to store coordinates as percentages of the image dimensions, where values are between 0 and 100, and convert to pixels only at render time using CSS positioning. This decision is important since it determines our entire data model and is the reason our annotations table stores x and y as REAL values rather than integers.

## Why This Matters for the Tech Stack

When a user clicks on the artwork and types a comment, the app needs to save that pin and show it on the image without refreshing the whole page. This requirement is what makes HTMX the most suitable option here, rather than a simpler approach, since it can send the data to the server in the background and drop the new pin straight onto the canvas.

## What Remains Uncertain

Our team still have one unresolved issue, which is finding a way to handle the annotation form UX. Option one is a pop-up that appears right on the image at the click point, which will feel intuitive because the comment is right next to the pin. Option two is a safer option of a sidebar panel that opens on the side, which is easier to navigate with a keyboard and works better for screen readers. Our approach is to make prototypes and see them directly to further decide.
